description Redcliff Pipe. The pipe or conduit supplied water to St. Mary Redcliffe Church. Rights over "Rugewell" were granted to the church by Robert Berkeley at the end of the twelfth century (Hirst 1924, 359-360) and the conduit pipe was presumably created after this grant. The source of the water supply is on the north side of Daventry Road, Knowle (at NGR ST 59667 70404) and is enclosed in a sunken chamber.
Beneath the northern end of the eighteenth century raised walkway at the western end of the churchyard was a conduit head. The recess containing the conduit was of one build with the balustrading above. The structure was of limestone ashlar jambs with a shallow arched head with projecting keystone. The conduit head was removed to near the south-west corner of the churchyard in 1832. A feather from the Redcliffe pipe, which ran across Redcliff Hill, supplied water to the Hospital of St. John the Baptist (BUAD 1055M) (Hobhouse 1887, 145).
During the Second World War bombing destroyed a stone chamber some 100 metres from the source of the spring in the Daventry Road area. An inspection chamber 156 metres to the north of this chamber through which the pipe ran was also destroyed by a bomb. A section of the pipe at the southern end of Redcliff Hill was also destroyed by bombing.
