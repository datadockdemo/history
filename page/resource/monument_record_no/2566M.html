description An earthwork feature located 60 metres to the north-east of the Purdown Anti-Aircraft Gun Battery site. It is sited on the eastern side of the Down, some 40 metres below the ridge. It is a low earth mound approximately 24.5 metres in diameter and survives to a height of roughly 0.25 metres. The character of the monument is at present unclear. It may be a barrow. However, O'Neil and Grinsell argue that it is not a long barrow and suggest that it "more probably a seventeenth-century mound connected with landscape gardening (O'Neil & Grinsell 1960, 68).
The feature and the surrounding area is currently under grass.
