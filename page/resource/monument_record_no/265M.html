description Three Cups and Salmon Public house, No.84 Redcliff Street, located on the south side of the junction of Pile Street and Redcliff Hill. The primary elevation of the building seems to have been that fronting on to Redcliff Hill. Two tenements stood on the site in 1591 and by 1659 the "Blue Boar" tavern had been constructed. At this time it was leased to and occupied by John Birdcombe, baker.
The building dated to the seventeenth century and was a gabled house of two storeys and attic. First-floor jettied, with one bracket; full-width pentice roofs above ground and first floors. Two right-hand entrances, windows to left. One bay each to upper storeys, the lower with an Ipswich window. Signs on front to left and right of first-floor window, below which reads another: "The Three Cups & Salmon Inn & Tavern by James Fuller Dealer in Foreign Spirits".
The building was demolished in 1843 to make way for the widening of Pile Street. The site is now beneath the footway in front of St. Mary Redcliff and southern side of the Redcliffe Way.
