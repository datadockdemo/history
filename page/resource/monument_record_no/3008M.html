description A fishpond towards the southern end of St Anne's Wood was in existance by the time of the Brislington tithe survey in 1846 (EP/A/32/9). The fishpond was fed by Brislington Brook and controlled by a sluice at the northern end of the pond marked on the Ordnance Survey 1:2500 plan (Somerset sheet VI.08) published in 1886. The pond measured approximately 120m north-south and 20m wide. It is likely to have been a feature of the designed 'picturesque' landscape for Wick House.
The pond was infilled prior to the First World War. Part of the sluice and associated stonework is still visible on both banks of the brook at NGR ST6200271496.
