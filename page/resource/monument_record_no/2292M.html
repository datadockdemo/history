description Barton Hill Primary School. The school was built on the site of Tilly's Court (2293M) in 1893-1894 (BRO Building Plan Books Vol.30 fol.57b). The school is a two-storey brick structure with limestonr ashlar detailing and a slate roof. The core of the structure is a central range, orientated from south-west to north-east, with cross-wings with stepped gables at either end. On the south-east facing (Queen Ann Road) frontage these enclose two-storey square towers with a two-window range and parapet, and at the centre of the elevation is a paired single-storey element with a three-window range. There is a tall brick stack at the centre of the valley between the two ranges. On the north-west facing elevation there are a series of attached single-storey structures with flat roofs which house offices and ancillary spaces.
The entrances to the school consisted of separate doors for girls and boys, the girls entrance being on the western side of the central ranges and the boys on the eastern side.
The entrances lead into the main hall which is open to the roof. The hall has pairs of classrooms off the eastern and western ends, stairs leading to the first floor on the southern side and offices and ancillary rooms on the northern side. A seventeenth century stone chimneypiece, originally from Tilly's Court (SMR 20187), is fixed to the centre of the southern wall of the hall while the office on the opposite side has a plaster ceiling which was also resited from Tilly's Court. This was cut into sections for removal and reassembled in its new location. At first floor level the stairs lead out on to a timber gallery with a balustrade along the southern side, eastern and western sides of the hall, giving access to the classrooms, and is supported by moulded brackets.
Covered single-storey playsheds with single-pitch roofs stand along the full length of both the north-eastern and south-western sides of the site.
