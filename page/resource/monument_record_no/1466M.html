description T. and J. Perry & Co. Carriageworks, No.104 (originally No.61) Stokes Croft, located on the eastern side of Stokes Croft. The carriage works was established in 1844 and consisted of a building which occupied the area between the Stokes Croft frontage and a lane off Hepburn Road to the rear. The original showroom was a two-storey rendered structure with a nine-window range. The ground-floor had a number of large vehicle arches beneath an open-sided lean-to roof supported by columns, which extended over the footway. The fenestration consisted of sash windows with stepped voussoirs above. The pitched roof, behind a parapet, had a bell cote at its centre.
The original building was damaged by fire and a new two-storey showroom was constructed to a design by the architect E. W. Godwin in 1858-1859 at a cost of �1000. This was added to existing buildings and Godwin apparently did not supervise the construction project (V & A AAD Ledger 4/9-1980: 9-10; Soros 1999, 193n).
The site was sold to Anderson's Rubber Company in 1912 and remained in its ownership until 1960. The building became vacant in 1979 and remained unused for thirty years.
The showroom has a Grade II* listing (Listed Building number 901-1/36/2037).
