description St. Mary-le-Port, a parish church located to on the south side of Mary-le-port Street.
Nave, chancel and north aisle. Church tower of C15. Limestone ashlar with coursed Pennant rubble. Perpendicular Gothic with a Somerset-type tower. 3 stages divided by drip moulds, with angle buttresses and an octagonal south-east stair tower. West door; 4-light window above, and a blind 2-light window in the second stage. Belfry with 2-light louvred windows, a crenellated parapet with corner pinnacles and a south-east spirelet. Unclerestoreyed 5-bay nave had one, north, aisle and 4-centred arches with moulded capitals. Stepped buttresses on south side. Chancel of 3 bays, and a small north-east chapel with 13th.century undercroft. South chancel windows of 3 lights in a square head.
Interior illustrated by Johnson in 1828 (M2791), showing the nave, looking east to the altar. Other artists drew the eagle lectern (M2794), a monument, monumental shields, and a coffin with well-preserved body found in 1814, (922A) (BRSMG M2793).
The main body of the church was destroyed by bombing during the Second World War. The footprint of the church was archaeologically excavated in 1962-63 by Philip Rahtz (Watts & Rahtz 1985).
