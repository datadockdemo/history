description Holy Cross Roman Catholic chapel in Victoria Street, opposite the junction with Avon Street. A temporary chapel was erected on the site in 1872 as a replacement for the chapel in Nelson's Gardens in Bedminster. A subscription was raised in 1893 to build a new church although only the west front and nave were completed. The church was dedicated on 2 December 1894 by Bishop Brownlow.
The building was a later ninteenth-century stone chapel with a pitched tiled roof. There was a large arched window in the centre of the upper part of the facade which was flanked by two smaller lancet windows. On either side of the entrance were two buttresses, the upper parts of which turned into pinnacles which projected above the roofline. There were two skylights on the north side of the roof.
The building was later converted into a car showroom and was demolished c.1940 to make way for the construction of Temple Way.
