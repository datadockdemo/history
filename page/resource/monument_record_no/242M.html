description Chatterton House Museum, located on the northern side of Pile Street. The house was constructed for the use of the master of Pile Street School in the mid-eighteenth century, and the building carries a date of 1749. The building is of two storeys and cellar with a hipped pantile roof and is constructed of rendered Pennant sandstone rubble. It has a three-window range and the main doorway has a flat canopy supported by scrolled brackets. A plaque above the door reads "THIS HOUSE WAS CREATED BY GILES MALPAS... AD 1749".
The building had a double-depth plan with connecting rooms behind a central staircase. The interior has, however, been significantly altered. There was a yard in front of the building enclosed by a screen wall of limestone ashlar with a doorway on the western side and two segmental-headed windows with architraves.
The father of the poet Thomas Chatterton was schoolmaster and Chatterton was born in the building in 1752. Chatterton was also educated at the school.
The screen wall was repositioned in 1937-1939 when Redcliffe Way was created. The house was opened as a museum, part of Bristol Museum and Art Gallery, in 1958 and was latterly used for staff accommodation. A porch was added to the building in the twentieth century and in 1983 the cellar was filled in. A plaque on the building commemmorates Thomas Chatterton and bears the legend "THE BIRTHPLACE OF THOMAS CHATTERTON" and the statue of Chatterton (BUAD 50M), which originally stood on a tall plinth outside St. Mary Redcliffe Church (BUAD 6M), is stored in the garden shed. The building was photographed in the 1880s (Winstone 1962, pl.63) and was drawn by Samuel Loxton in early twentieth century (Bristol Reference Library V1218 and V1219. It was surveyed in 1980 (BUAD 930).
The building has a Grade II listing (Listed Building number 901-1/42/235).
