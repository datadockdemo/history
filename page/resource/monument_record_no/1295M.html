description No.24 Rosemary Street. The building was a house of probable seventeenth-century date which lay on the north side of Rosemary Street. The building appears to have been built as a one of a terrace of four and was a one window range house of two storeys plus attic with a gabled roof. The entrance on the ground floor was on the west side of the frontage. The building shared a lateral rubble stack with No.22. The building was gutted as a result of enemy action in June 1941 and the shell was demolished for the development of the Broadmead shopping centre, probably in the winter of 1954-1955.
The remains of the building are likely to be fairly well-preserved below ground.
