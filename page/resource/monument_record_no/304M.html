description "The Palace," located on the south side of the Pile Street, towards Temple Gate. The building was a lodge or garden house, probably extant by c.1660 but possibly a conversion of an earlier building, which stood at he north-east corner of a walled garden of approximately one acre.
The building was a single-bay gabled house of two storeys plus attic and was of stone rubble construction. There was a small window at ground floor in the centre of the elevation. A first-floor oriel took up about half of the total height of the wall. It was canted and the lower part had four wooden panels on the front, and one on each side. Above there was an eight-light transomed window, with two-lights on each side and the oriel had a pantile roof. There was also a small two-light window in immediately beneath the eaves of the gable end. The gabled roof was pantiled and there was a pendant at the apex of the eaves. On the east wall of the building there was an attached structure just over one storey in height with a lean-to pantile roof. The plot was bounded by a single-storey stone rubble wall attached to the north-west corner of the building, which ran parallel with Pile Street. There was a doorway in this wall immediately to the west of the building.
The building was recorded as the lodge where Richard Novis lived. It was occupied by John Vittle and then by Charles Weekes , gardener, and the house and garden was granted by Henry Rich to Judith Rich in 1691.
G. W. Braikenridge noted in 1821 that the house possessed a small garden behind, implying that part of the had been taken into a different ownership by that date. Braikenridge also reported that the building belonged to Messrs. Henry Ricketts & Co. at the Glass House, and was due to be removed.
