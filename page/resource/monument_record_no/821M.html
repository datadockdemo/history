description The Carpenters Arms public house, No.13 St. James' Churchyard. The building was a gabled, jettied house of two storeys and attic with a 1-window range. It was of probable seventeenth-century date. The footprint of the building measured by 7 metres from north-west to south-east by approximately 4.25 metres wide. The main elevation faced west on to Haymarket and there was a central projecting bay. The fenestration at first-floor and attic level comprised sashes. The gabled roof was covered with clay tiles and the upper part of the gable appears to have been cut back to form a small hip. A painted signboard which projected slightly beyond the eaves was fixed to to the roof at the base of the hip. A substantial chimney stack, apparently shared with No.12 St. James' Churchyard attached to the south (BUAD 820M ), projected from the roof on the southern side of the building.
The building was demolished in 1953 or 1954 and its site now lies beneath the highway in The Haymarket.
